Samsung reveals first eco-friendly graphene battery for smartphones

The revolutionary technology promises longer duration, ultra-fast charging and a significantly reduced environmental impact in the mobile industry

Samsung Electronics has officially presented its revolutionary eco-friendly graphene battery, the first of its kind designed specifically for smartphones. This innovation promises to transform the mobile industry with technology that combines superior energy performance with a firm commitment to environmental sustainability.

"This graphene battery represents the sustainable future of mobile technology. It not only doubles the duration and charging speed, but drastically reduces our carbon footprint." - DJ Koh, Samsung Mobile CEO

The new battery uses graphene obtained through 100% sustainable processes, eliminating the use of traditional toxic materials like cobalt and significantly reducing CO2 emissions during its manufacturing. According to Samsung, this technology can charge a smartphone to 80% in just 12 minutes and maintain more than 95% of its capacity after 1,500 charging cycles.

The first independent analyses confirm that the graphene battery offers 45% superior energy density compared to conventional lithium batteries, while its production process generates 60% fewer polluting emissions. Additionally, the materials are completely recyclable at the end of their useful life.

The most innovative features include ultra-fast wireless charging at 50W, self-repair technology that extends lifespan, and an intelligent thermal management system that maintains optimal temperatures during intensive use.

Samsung plans to implement this technology in its upcoming Galaxy S26 line, which will launch in the first quarter of 2026. The company also announced that it will license the technology to other manufacturers to accelerate the mass adoption of sustainable batteries in the industry.
