Apple launches iPhone 17 with integrated neural chip and holographic display

By Jane Doe | May 24, 2025

Apple has unveiled the iPhone 17, a groundbreaking device featuring a built-in neural processing chip and a fully functional holographic display. This innovation marks a significant leap forward in smartphone technology, blending artificial intelligence with cutting-edge visual interfaces.

During the company’s Spring Event, CEO Tim Cook showcased the iPhone 17’s capabilities, including real-time language translation, predictive interface adaptation, and 3D holographic calling. The neural chip, dubbed “Aion,” is designed to process contextual data on-device, enhancing privacy and responsiveness.

Analysts say the iPhone 17 could redefine how users interact with mobile devices, moving beyond touch and voice into spatial interaction. The holographic display uses micro-lens projection and AI-driven rendering to create vivid, floating visuals visible without headsets.

Pre-orders begin next week, with prices starting at $1,499. The device will ship in three finishes: graphite, starlight, and a new iridescent hue called “Spectra.”
